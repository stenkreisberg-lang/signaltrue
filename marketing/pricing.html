Frequently asked questions

Why is baseline calibration required?

SignalTrue detects deviation from your organization's normal patterns, not absolute thresholds. Without a stable baseline, every metric is just a number with no context. Calibration builds the foundation for accurate risk detection and prevents false alarms.

Why don't I see recommendations immediately?

During baseline calibration (first 6 weeks), SignalTrue is learning what "normal" looks like for your teams. Risk signals require comparison against a stable baseline. Showing signals before calibration completes would create noise and erode trust in the system.

How is this different from pulse surveys?

Pulse surveys ask people how they feel. SignalTrue observes behavioral patterns that precede burnout and overload—often before people self-report issues. Surveys are lagging indicators. Behavioral signals are leading indicators.

Is this employee monitoring?

No. SignalTrue never tracks individual productivity, message content, or person-by-person behavior. All signals are computed at team level (minimum 8 members) using only metadata. It detects system-level risk, not individual performance.

What counts as an active user?

An active user is a team member whose collaboration metadata (calendar and Slack) contributes to team-level signals during the billing period. Inactive, disconnected, or archived users are not billed.

Can I pause or cancel anytime?

Yes. You can pause or cancel at any time. Your baseline data is retained for 90 days in case you decide to resume. After 90 days, data is permanently deleted per our retention policy.
