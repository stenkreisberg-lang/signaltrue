What changes

Top 20% of team members carrying increasing share of collaboration activity. Participation concentration often precedes disengagement.

Why it matters

Sustained imbalance creates capacity risk for high contributors and withdrawal risk for others. Team friction and disengagement often appear within 3-8 weeks.

What leaders can do

  • Rotate ownership for recurring meetings and updates
  • Reduce meeting attendance sprawl. Clarify owners
  • Audit workload distribution and decision rights

How benchmarking works

Default: Self-comparison

SignalTrue benchmarks teams against their own historical baseline by default. You see deviation from your organization's normal patterns, not industry averages.

This prevents false positives from comparing fast-moving startups to established enterprises, or distributed teams to co-located ones.

Optional: External context

External benchmarks are available as optional context, not performance scores. They help answer "Is this unusual for teams like ours?" but never replace self-comparison as the primary signal.

External benchmarks are role-based (engineering, sales, support) and size-adjusted (8-15, 16-30, 31+ members).

Privacy by design

How aggregation works and why it protects trust

What data is used

  • Calendar event metadata (start/end times, attendee count, organizer flag)
  • Slack message metadata (timestamp, channel type, thread structure)
  • No message content, no file contents, no individual work artifacts

What is never used

  • Message or email content
  • File contents or document text
  • Keystroke or screen activity
  • Individual productivity scores

How aggregation works

  • All signals computed at team level (minimum 8 active members)
  • Individual data never shown to managers or leaders
  • Patterns require 60% data coverage to prevent sparse-data leakage

Why this protects trust

  • Leaders see system issues, not individual behavior
  • No incentive for micromanagement or surveillance
  • Teams understand the system detects overload, not poor performance
